The Ambiguity Watch — included with Pro and above

The clear rules are easy.
The grey ones are where the bills come from.

Most regulatory changes are clear, and Gubernis tells you about them — quickly, in plain English, dispatched the way you read best. That’s most of the work.

But the rules that catch people out aren’t the clear ones. They’re the ones where two careful readings of the same published text produce two different answers. A new BIS amendment that could be a clarification or a substantive expansion. A CFSP decision that overlaps existing measures in unexpected ways. A corrigendum that might be a typo fix or might be something more.

Gubernis reads each change through multiple independent paths. Where the readings diverge — where the rule itself is ambiguous — we flag it. The Ambiguity Watch surfaces not just what changed, but where the change is grey.

Because the rule worth taking to your counsel isn’t the one we all read the same way. It’s the one we don’t.

What Gubernis does

Watches the regulatory wires so you can stay focused on the work.

Trade regulation is in motion every working day. BIS amends an export-control entry. HMRC posts a tariff notice. The EU Council publishes a CFSP decision. OFAC adds entities to the SDN list. Each one matters to somebody — and the people it matters to typically find out when something has already broken.

Gubernis watches the public regulatory wires — UK, EU, US, and growing — and surfaces the changes that matter. Each change is summarised in plain English, dated, and dispatched to you in the form you read it best: a weekly digest, an instant alert, or an API call into your workflow.

We don’t pattern-match keywords. We map every publication against a structured trade-compliance ontology — HS chapters, ECCN entries, sanctions programmes, jurisdictions, industry verticals. So when the wire moves, we already know which sections of your work it touches.

We don’t make compliance decisions for you. We don’t produce auto-recommendations. We surface the change, summarise it cleanly, and put it in front of you in time to think. That’s the whole product.
